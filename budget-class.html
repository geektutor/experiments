üéØ Facilitator Guide

Questions to Ask (Not Tell)

  • "What makes that a NEED vs. a WANT?"
  • "Could that expense be reduced without eliminating it?"
  • "What would happen if you cut that completely?"
  • "Where's the most room to adjust?"
  • "Is this sustainable for 6 months?"
  • "What's the opportunity cost of this expense?"

Red Flags to Watch For

  • Math doesn't add up to 100% of income
  • "Needs" category is 70%+ (probably miscategorized)
  • Zero money allocated for savings/debt
  • Unrealistic cuts ("Just stop eating out forever!")
  • Ignoring the scenario's specific challenge

Common Debate Topics

  • Gym Membership: Need for mental health vs. Want for lifestyle?
  • Eating Out: Where's the line between social needs and indulgence?
  • Family Support: Obligation vs. enabling - how much is reasonable?
  • Tithe/Church: Personal values vs. financial pressure
  • Car vs. Public Transport: Convenience vs. cost

Facilitation Techniques

  • Silent Observation: Let the group struggle a bit before jumping in
  • Socratic Method: Answer questions with questions
  • Real Talk: Share your own money struggles to build trust
  • Challenge Assumptions: "Says who?" is a powerful question
  • Acknowledge Difficulty: "This is hard - you're doing great"
Remember: There's no ONE right answer. Different groups will budget differently based on their values and priorities. Your job is to make them THINK, not to give them the "correct" budget.
