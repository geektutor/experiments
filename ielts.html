Part 2: Reading (30 minutes)

Read the passage and answer questions 1-20

The Impact of Artificial Intelligence on Modern Workplaces

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is revolutionizing the modern workplace in unprecedented ways. From automation of routine tasks to advanced data analysis, AI technologies are transforming how businesses operate and how employees perform their jobs. This transformation brings both opportunities and challenges that organizations must navigate carefully.

One of the most significant impacts of AI is the automation of repetitive tasks. Machine learning algorithms can now handle data entry, basic customer service inquiries, and even some aspects of financial analysis. This automation frees human workers to focus on more creative and strategic activities that require emotional intelligence and complex decision-making skills. For instance, while AI can process thousands of customer complaints to identify patterns, human managers are still needed to develop innovative solutions and implement organizational changes.

However, the integration of AI also raises concerns about job displacement. Studies suggest that up to 30% of jobs could be automated by 2030, particularly in manufacturing, transportation, and customer service sectors. This potential displacement necessitates significant investment in retraining and upskilling programs to help workers transition to new roles that complement AI technologies rather than compete with them.

Moreover, AI is creating entirely new job categories. Data scientists, AI ethicists, machine learning engineers, and algorithm auditors are just a few examples of emerging professions. These roles require a combination of technical expertise and domain knowledge, highlighting the importance of continuous learning in the AI era. Organizations that invest in developing their workforce's AI literacy are likely to have a competitive advantage.

The ethical implications of AI in the workplace cannot be ignored. Issues such as algorithmic bias, privacy concerns, and the transparency of AI decision-making processes require careful consideration. Companies must establish clear guidelines and governance frameworks to ensure that AI systems are used responsibly and do not perpetuate existing inequalities or create new forms of discrimination.
